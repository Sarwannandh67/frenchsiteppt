French History Timeline

Explore the major events that shaped France from ancient Gaul to modern times

58-50 BCE

Conquest of Gaul

Julius Caesar conquers Gaul, bringing the region under Roman control. The decisive Battle of Alesia (52 BCE) marks the defeat of Vercingetorix and the Gallic tribes.

486 CE

Clovis I Unites the Franks

Clovis I becomes the first king to unite all Frankish tribes, establishing the Merovingian dynasty and converting to Christianity.

800 CE

Charlemagne Crowned Emperor

Charlemagne (Charles the Great) is crowned Emperor of the Romans by Pope Leo III, creating what would later be known as the Holy Roman Empire.

1337-1453

The Hundred Years' War

A series of conflicts between England and France over succession to the French throne. Joan of Arc emerges as a pivotal figure, helping turn the tide in favor of France.

16th Century

French Renaissance

France experiences a cultural and artistic revival, with the spread of Renaissance ideas and humanist philosophy transforming art, literature, and intellectual thought.

1643-1715

Reign of Louis XIV

The "Sun King" rules for 72 years, the longest reign in European history. He centralizes power, builds the Palace of Versailles, and establishes France as the dominant European power.

1789-1799

French Revolution

A period of radical social and political upheaval that overthrows the monarchy, establishes a republic, and dramatically transforms French society.

1804-1815

Napoleonic Era

Napoleon Bonaparte crowns himself Emperor and dominates European affairs until his final defeat at the Battle of Waterloo in 1815.

1870-1940

Third Republic

France's longest-lasting constitutional system, established after the fall of Napoleon III. This period includes the Belle Ã‰poque, World War I, and the interwar years.

1940-1944

Vichy France & Resistance

During World War II, France is occupied by Nazi Germany. The Vichy regime collaborates with the occupiers while the Resistance fights against them.

1944-1958

Fourth Republic

Post-war reconstruction and the beginning of decolonization, including the First Indochina War and the start of the Algerian War.

1958-Present

Fifth Republic

Established by Charles de Gaulle, this constitutional system continues to the present day. Key events include May 1968 protests, the end of the colonial empire, and France's role in European integration.
